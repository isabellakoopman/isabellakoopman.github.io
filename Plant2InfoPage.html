The Story of Autumn Sage

When summer ends and most flowers grow tired, Autumn Sage begins her show. Bright red trumpets bloom just as the air turns crisp along the path.

Hummingbirds race to her. “Everyone else is quiet,” they say. “But you’re still singing.” “I saved my song for now,” Sage replies, swaying in the cool light.

She offers nectar when other flowers have closed. The birds sip gratefully, carrying pollen from trumpet to trumpet. Tiny bees join the chorus, buzzing soft and low.

Why bloom so late?” asks the grass nearby. “Because friends need food in every season,” Sage answers. “Community means sharing at the moment it matters most.”

About This Community Member

Scientific Name: Quercus velutina.

Appearance: Tall tree with dark, ridged bark and shiny green leaves that turn orange-yellow in fall. Produces acorns with scaly cups.

Ecological Role: Provides acorns that feed deer, squirrels, mice, turkeys, and blue jays. Forgotten acorns sprout into new oaks.

Fun Fact: Black Oak bark was once used to make dyes and tannins for leather.
